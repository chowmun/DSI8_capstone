Exploratory Data Analysis

Data Overview

The datasets we used is prepared by Zygmunt Zajac. It consists of 6 million ratings from 50k of users on 10k popular books from Goodreads.
The datasets contains 5csv files but we mainly use rating.csv (rating of users) and books.csv(metadata for each user)
Figure beside shows the rating distribution of book rating from rating.csv. In general, number of ratings are increasing with the positive rating itself.



Data Cleaning

The combination of books and users are large and therefore may cause memory issues. We need to filter and extract appropriate data for the recommender system.

To filter the books selection, we plotted Graph 1 to show the rating frequency(number of ratings) for all books. We notice that Graph 1 exhibits a long-tail property pattern in which only a small fraction of items(popular books) are rated frequently. This causes a highly skewed distribution of the ratings.

Then, we plot another graph using the same data but in a log scale as shown in Graph 2. We set the threshold at 500, in which the selected books must be rated at least 500 times.

Based on the rating frequency in Graph 3, the users were filtered. We only consider user who rated at least 117 books.. After all the filtering process, we selected 2526 books and 21557 users for our recommender system.
